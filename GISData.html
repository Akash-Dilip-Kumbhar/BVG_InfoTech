Why GIS & Remote Sensing In Agriculture

  • Near to real time crop monitoring system can be developed using RS & GIS.
  • Reliable & timely information on types of crops grown, their area and expected yield.
  • Visualisation of crop distribution across the region through remote sensing data.
  • Prediction of crop sowing & harvesting date by using satellite remote sensing data.
  • Crop acreage estimation through image classification techniques
  • Crop condition monitoring through spectral indices like NDVI, NDWI
  • Useful for monitoring of area & production, yield forecast, managing resources, reduction of input cost & prediction of future outcomes.
  • Compilation is accurate & much faster compared to traditional methods.
  • It will provide support to policy makers for decision making.
